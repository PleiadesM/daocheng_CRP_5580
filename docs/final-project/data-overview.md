# MovieMap — Data

This directory holds every dataset used by the *MovieMap* scrollytelling site and
its embedded Tableau dashboards. Each CSV here was either downloaded directly
from a public open-data portal or scraped from a public web source and cleaned
into a wide- or long-format table that Tableau can consume.

```
data/
├── sf_film/        San Francisco filming locations + Film SF impact reports
├── hengdian/       Hengdian World Studios — attractions, productions, photos, shapefile
└── imdb/           IMDb Top 250 metadata + a 91-movie location corpus
```

The site uses each dataset as follows:

| Site section | Data used |
| --- | --- |
| SF filming-locations map (Section 01) | `sf_film/Film_Locations_in_San_Francisco_20260510.csv` |
| SF film-economy dashboard (Section 01) | the seven other CSVs in `sf_film/` |
| Hengdian theme-park map (Section 01) | `hengdian/hengdian_attractions.csv` + `hengdian/shapefiles/` |
| Hengdian productions (tooltip, Section 01) | `hengdian/hengdian_productions.csv` |
| IMDb genre / box-office dashboard (Section 02) | `imdb/imdb_top250_clean.csv` |
| 3D Globe Explorer (Section 03) | `imdb/movie_location_corpus/` |

## `sf_film/` — San Francisco

### `Film_Locations_in_San_Francisco_20260510.csv` (2,214 rows)

Every permitted filming location in the City of San Francisco, with the
production's title, year, fun-fact note, and a `POINT(lng lat)` geocode.

- **Source:** DataSF — *Film Locations in San Francisco*
  <https://data.sfgov.org/Culture-and-Recreation/Film-Locations-in-San-Francisco/yitu-d5am>
- **Snapshot date:** 2026-05-10 (filename suffix)
- **Grain:** one (title × location) pair

### Film SF annual impact reports (six CSVs)

Scraped from the four annual impact reports published by **Film SF**, the San
Francisco Office of Economic and Workforce Development's film commission, and
broken out into one table per metric family. `fiscal_year` is the calendar year
in which the fiscal year ends (so `2025` = FY24-25 = July 2024 – June 2025).

| File | Grain | Columns of note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `filming_statistics.csv` (4 rows) | one fiscal year | direct economic output, permit fees, shoot days, productions |
| `funding.csv` (4 rows) | one fiscal year | permit fees, general fund, Grants for the Arts, total budget |
| `productions_by_type.csv` (44 rows) | fiscal year × production type | productions, shoot days per type (WEB, FEATURE, TV SERIES, etc.) |
| `jurisdictions.csv` (4 rows) | one fiscal year | shoot days by jurisdiction (Rec & Parks, Presidio, GG NRA, SFO, …) |
| `crew_hotel_sagaftra.csv` (12 rows) | fiscal year × category | crew hires, SF hotel nights, local SAG-AFTRA hires |
| `notable_productions.csv` (64 rows) | one notable production | title, director, starring, network/studio |

**Source documents:**

| Fiscal year | URL |
| --- | --- |
| 2025 | <https://media.api.sf.gov/documents/SF_Film_Annual_Report_FY24-25_Final.pdf> |
| 2024 | <https://media.api.sf.gov/documents/SF_Film_Annual_Report_FY23-24_2.26.pdf> |
| 2023 | <https://www.sf.gov/sites/default/files/2024-01/Film%20SF%20Impact%20Report%20FY22_23.pdf> |
| 2022 | <https://www.sf.gov/sites/default/files/2023-05/Film%20SF%20Annual%20Report%20FY21-22_1.pdf> |

**Caveats:**
- `direct_economic_output_usd` is blank for FY21-22; Film SF didn't headline that
  metric until FY22-23.
- 2024's `productions_by_type` shoot-day sum is 507 vs. the headline 506
  (rounding in the source).
- `california_film_commission` and `sfo` columns in `jurisdictions.csv` are
  blank for FY21-22 through FY23-24; they were added in the FY24-25 report.

## `hengdian/` — Hengdian World Studios

### `hengdian_attractions.csv` (7 rows)

The seven major themed environments / studio sets that make up Hengdian World
Studios, each tagged with the role it plays (scenic spot, shooting base, live
show, etc.).

### `hengdian_productions.csv` (30 rows)

Films and TV series filmed at Hengdian, with director, cast, box office (films
only), Wikipedia URL, downloaded poster path, and a heuristic mapping to one or
more of the seven attractions.

- **Source:** English Wikipedia — *Hengdian World Studios* and each production's
  individual article. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hengdian_World_Studios>
- ⚠️ **Caveat on `filming_attractions`:** Wikipedia does not publish a
  production-to-set mapping for Hengdian. The values in this column are
  **heuristic matches** generated by mapping each story's historical era to the
  attraction whose architectural style recreates that era (e.g., Qing-dynasty
  palace dramas → *Palace of Ming and Qing Dynasties*; 1840s–1949 Shanghai
  settings → *Legend of Bund*). The `match_basis` column records the reasoning
  for each row. Two modern-set productions (*DOA: Dead or Alive*, *No Return*)
  are intentionally blank because they don't map cleanly to any historical-era
  attraction.

### `hengdian_visitor_attendance.csv` (9 rows)

Annual visitor count to Hengdian World Studios, in millions, for 2011 – 2019.
Source: Wikipedia. Later years are not currently reported on the article.

### `attraction_photos.csv` + `attraction_photos/` (12 photos)

Wikimedia Commons photos of Hengdian attractions, with OSM ID, attraction name,
local file path, original URL, license, and credit. All photos are licensed
**CC BY-SA 4.0** (or compatible); see the `license` and `credit` columns.

### `posters/` (29 poster images)

Poster images for the productions in `hengdian_productions.csv`, downloaded
from each production's English Wikipedia infobox. Used under fair use for
non-commercial educational presentation. Two productions in
`hengdian_productions.csv` are missing posters (Iron Road has no infobox image
on Wikipedia; one other was unavailable at scrape time).

### `shapefiles/` — `HengdianStudio.shp` (+ companions)

A polygon shapefile of the seven Hengdian attractions used to render the theme
parks on the Tableau map.

- **Source:** OpenStreetMap (Hengdian Studios features, exported via QGIS).
- **Companion files:** `.dbf` (attribute table), `.prj` (projection),
  `.shx` (index), `.cpg` (encoding), `.qgz` (QGIS project file used to author
  the export).

## `imdb/` — IMDb Top 250

### `imdb_top250_clean.csv` (499 rows)

IMDb Top 250 metadata enriched with up to 11 filming-region tags per title and
a worldwide box-office figure pulled from each film's Wikipedia article.

| Column group | Columns |
| --- | --- |
| Identity | `imdb_id`, `top250_rank`, `title`, `year` |
| Reception | `imdb_rating`, `votes` |
| Production | `runtime_minutes`, `genre`, `director`, `cast` |
| Locations | `region_1` … `region_11` (US state or country names) |
| Money | `box_office_usd`, `box_office_raw`, `box_office_source` |

- **Source — Top 250 list and metadata:** <https://www.imdb.com/chart/top/>
- **Source — box office:** linked Wikipedia article for each film
  (`box_office_source` column).

### `movie_location_corpus/` — filming-location corpus (91 movies)

A scraped corpus of detailed filming-location pages for 91 of the Top 250
films, used by the 3D Globe Explorer.

| Path | Contents |
| --- | --- |
| `corpus_index.csv` (91 rows) | one row per movie — IMDb ID, title, year, source URL, paths to cleaned HTML / JSON, director, locations, image count |
| `corpus.jsonl` | one JSON record per movie, mirroring the index |
| `html/` (91 files) | a cleaned-up HTML rendering of each movie's location page, used inside the explorer's poster sidebar |
| `json/` (91 files) | the same data in structured form |
| `assets/` (one folder per IMDb ID) | locally cached poster + scene images |

- **Source:** *The Worldwide Guide to Movie Locations* — <http://www.movie-locations.com/>
  (specifically the per-movie pages linked from the Top 250 index).

## Reproducibility

Most of the scraping and cleaning steps that produced these CSVs are in the
project's `from-scratch/` and `data_source/` directories at the repo root —
notably `data_source/build_imdb_clean.py`, `data_source/hengdian/build_hengdian.py`,
and `data_source/hengdian/match_attractions.py`. They use `requests` +
`beautifulsoup4` and identify themselves with a polite User-Agent. Run dates
are recorded in each CSV's snapshot suffix where applicable.
